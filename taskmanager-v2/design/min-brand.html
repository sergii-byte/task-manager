02

construction.

how the mark is built
type Inter Tight 700, lowercase, tracking −0.045 em. The descender on y drops 0.32 em — deeper than default — so the plum letter holds visual weight against the larger ink word.
the dot A circle 0.18× cap-height, sitting on the baseline, offset 0.05 em from the y. It is the only ornamental element in the mark. It reads as a period — a sentence that has ended cleanly.
colour Black at #0a0a0a, never pure black. Plum at #6a1b9a only on the y. The dot is muted grey #c9c6bd — never plum, never ink. It must look said, not stamped.

07

principles.

six rules · in order of priority
01

paper, not panel.

Surfaces read like edited documents, not configured dashboards. White space is content. Containers are the exception, not the default.

02

one accent, used once.

Plum appears once per screen — on the thing that matters most. If everything is plum, nothing is. The dot is the only exception.

03

lowercase, deliberate.

All copy is lowercase except proper nouns. not a stylistic affectation — it's the volume control. ordify never raises its voice.

04

the rail is a voice.

The ai-rail is conversation, not toolbar. It writes in second person, sentence form, and shows its work. It says i, never we.

05

numbers are sacred.

Money, hours, ids — always JetBrains Mono, always tabular nums. never break alignment. €4,719.00 is the same width as €1,500.00.

06

show the impact, then save.

Every change ordify proposes shows what shifts before commit. Settings preview rate-bump impact. Drafts preview send. Confidence is shown, not hidden.
